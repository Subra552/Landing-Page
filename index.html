Pepsi was introduced to the market in 1893 as “Brad’s Drink”. The founder of Pepsi-Cola, Caleb Bradham, was a drug store owner. They initially intended the drink for people with digestion problems or stomachache. The name “Pepsi-Cola” was a derivative from pepsin, a digestive enzyme, kola nuts, and vanilla and sugar.
